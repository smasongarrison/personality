Data Science for Psychologists

Don’t Miss Module 00

This overview is designed to orient you to the class. Please watch the videos from this playlist and work your way through the notes. Although the module-level playlists are embedded in the course, you can find the full-course video playlist here. In addition, you can find the slides for this module here. Currently, there are 7 videos in this playlist. The average video length is 12 minutes, 27 seconds. The total length of the playlist is 1 hour, 27 minutes, 10 seconds.

Data Science for Psychologists (CN4P) introduces on the principles of data science, including:

  • data wrangling,
  • modeling,
  • visualization, and
  • communication.

In this class, we link those principles to psychological methods and open science practices by emphasizing exploratory analyses and description, rather than confirmatory analyses and prediction. Through the semester we will work our way thru Wickham and Grolemund’s R for Data Science text and develop proficiency with tidyverse. This class emphasizes replication and reproducibility. CN4P is a practical skilled-based class and should be useful to students aiming for academia as well as those interested in industry. Applications of these methods can be applied to a full range of psychological areas, including perception (e.g, eye-tracking data), neuroscience (e.g., visualizing neural networks), and individual differences (e.g., valence analysis).

0.1 Big Ideas

This class covers the following broad five areas:

  • Reproducibility;
  • Replication;
  • Robust Methods;
  • Resplendent Visualizations; and
  • R Programming.
